One Africa Alliance Program for People Living with Disabilities

Empowering Abilities, Building Inclusive Futures

Across Africa, millions of people live with disabilities, yet face barriers that limit their access to education, employment, healthcare, and full participation in society. The One Africa Alliance Program for People Living with Disabilities (PLWD) is dedicated to breaking down these barriers, fostering inclusion, and promoting equal rights and opportunities.

Our mission is to empower PLWD through advocacy, inclusive policies, accessible infrastructure, and community-driven initiatives that enable dignity, independence, and social integration.

Why Inclusion of People Living with Disabilities Matters

Inclusion is a fundamental human right and essential for social justice and economic development. When people with disabilities are supported and included, communities thrive, economies grow, and societies become more resilient.

Yet, stigma, discrimination, and lack of resources continue to exclude many. Our program envisions a continent where all people, regardless of ability, can live with dignity and contribute fully.

Our Core Focus Areas

1. Advocacy and Rights Protection

  • Championing policies that protect the rights of PLWD.
  • Working with governments and organizations to implement inclusive legislation.
  • Raising awareness to combat stigma and discrimination.

2. Inclusive Education and Skills Development

  • Promoting accessible schools and learning materials.
  • Providing vocational training and life skills programs.
  • Supporting assistive technologies for learners with disabilities.

3. Accessible Infrastructure and Services

  • Ensuring public spaces, transport, and buildings are accessible.
  • Improving access to healthcare tailored to the needs of PLWD.
  • Facilitating access to assistive devices and rehabilitation services.

4. Economic Empowerment and Employment

  • Creating opportunities for PLWD to start and grow businesses.
  • Partnering with employers to foster inclusive workplaces.
  • Providing microfinance and support for entrepreneurship.

5. Community Engagement and Support Networks

  • Building peer support groups and community forums.
  • Engaging families and caregivers in empowerment initiatives.
  • Promoting leadership roles for PLWD in communities.

Our Key Programs

Rights and Inclusion Advocacy Network

Driving legislative change and community awareness campaigns across African countries.

Inclusive Education Initiative

Providing resources, training, and technology to make education accessible for all learners.

Accessible Africa Project

Upgrading public infrastructure and services to meet accessibility standards continent-wide.

Economic Empowerment Hub

Supporting entrepreneurs with disabilities through training, funding, and mentorship.

Community Inclusion Circles

Building local support networks to foster inclusion, leadership, and wellbeing.

How You Can Help Build an Inclusive Africa

  • Individuals & Families: Support PLWD in your communities, challenge stigma, and advocate for inclusion.
  • Youth & Students: Volunteer, promote accessibility, and participate in awareness programs.
  • Employers & Businesses: Create inclusive workplaces and provide opportunities to PLWD.
  • Governments & Policymakers: Enforce disability rights laws and invest in accessibility infrastructure.
  • Donors & Partners: Fund initiatives that empower PLWD and promote systemic change.

Our Vision for an Africa Where Everyone Thrives

  • All people living with disabilities enjoy equal rights, dignity, and opportunity.
  • Inclusive education and accessible environments become the norm.
  • Economic and social barriers are dismantled for full participation.
  • Communities celebrate diversity and empower PLWD as leaders and change-makers.
